About

I'm an undergraduate student studying Computer Science at the University of Virginia (Go Hoos!). I am interested in artificial intelligence (AI), and particularly its interactions with humans on a physiological level as a means to improve health and society. Currently, Voice AI and sensoring tools especially excite me. This passion was sparked by my fascination with technology and a lasting childhood interest in sports and fitness. Recently, this interest has matured into growing curiosity about machine learning applications in healthcare. I remain curious about machine learning in other domains (i.e., finance sectors).

Previously, I was a researcher at Sunnybrook Research Institute where I successfully tailored existing drug cardiotoxicity predictor (DICT) models for oncology drugs, drastically improving performance. As first-author, my team and I were accepted into AACR Special Conference: AI and Machine Learning 2025 where I delivered my first poster presentation!

Outside of school and research, I'm passionate about weightlifting, cooking, and basketball. Additionally, I enjoy exploring all kinds of new music!
